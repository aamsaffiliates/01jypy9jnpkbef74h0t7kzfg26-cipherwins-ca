GAMBLING SELF-ASSESSMENT

Do You Have a Gambling Problem?

Answer these questions honestly to assess your gambling habits. This test is confidential and designed to help you recognize potential warning signs.

1. Do you often gamble longer than you originally planned?

2. Do you often gamble until your last dollar is gone?

3. Have you ever lied to people important to you about how much you gambled?

4. Do you feel you have ever had a problem with gambling?

5. Do you gamble to escape from problems or when feeling depressed or anxious?

6. Have you ever borrowed money or sold anything to get money to gamble?

7. Do you feel anxious or irritable when trying to cut down on gambling?

HOW TO KEEP GAMBLING FUN

DO's - Keep It Fun

  • Set a Budget Before You Play

    Decide how much you can afford to lose and stick to it

  • Set Time Limits

    Use alarms or casino tools to limit your playing time

  • Take Regular Breaks

    Step away from the game every 30-60 minutes

  • Play for Entertainment

    View gambling as paying for entertainment, not making money

  • Keep Track of Your Spending

    Monitor your deposits and losses regularly

  • Maintain Other Hobbies

    Keep gambling as one of many entertainment options

DON'Ts - Warning Signs

  • Never Chase Your Losses

    Don't increase bets to try to win back money

  • Don't Gamble When Upset

    Avoid gambling when angry, depressed, or stressed

  • Don't Borrow Money to Gamble

    Never use credit cards, loans, or borrowed funds

  • Don't Gamble Under the Influence

    Alcohol and drugs impair judgment

  • Don't Lie About Your Gambling

    Hiding gambling activity is a warning sign

  • Don't Neglect Responsibilities

    Never let gambling interfere with work, family, or bills

WHEN THE FUN STOPS, STOP

Time Signals

  • Playing longer than planned
  • Losing track of time
  • Skipping meals or sleep

Money Signals

  • Spending more than budgeted
  • Chasing losses
  • Using bill money

Emotional Signals

  • Feeling anxious or stressed
  • Getting angry at losses
  • No longer enjoying the game
